## When to Use Each Filter

Choosing the right filter is half the creative work. Here is a quick guide:

* **Grayscale (Black and White):** Best for dramatic portraits, architecture, and minimalist layouts where composition and contrast should do the talking. Pair with our [Grayscale Converter](/tools/grayscale-converter) if you need fine-tuned brightness and contrast sliders.
* **Sepia:** Ideal for nostalgic blog headers, heritage branding, and warm editorial photography that evokes aged print.
* **Vintage:** Great for social posts, mood boards, and lifestyle content where muted saturation and soft warmth feel more authentic than heavy color grading.
* **Blur:** Use for soft backgrounds behind text overlays, simulating shallow depth of field, or de-emphasizing busy backgrounds in product shots.
* **Vignette:** Perfect for drawing the eye toward a subject in portraits, hero banners, and thumbnail images where the center should feel brighter than the edges.
